LLM recommendation

View Repository

Overview

Explored the efficacy of integrating Affordance-based Deep Q-Learning (DQN) reinforcement learning techniques with Large Language Models (LLMs) to enhance sequential recommendation systems. This project aimed to generalize recommendations efficienctly by modelling sequential tasks as Markov Decision Processes (MDP), thereby reducing dependency on extensive fine-tuning of LLMs.

Key Contributions

  • Reinforcement Learning Integration: Investigated both traditional Deep Q-Learning (DQN) and affordance-based Deep Q-Learning (ADQN), evaluating their performance against fine-tuned LLM benchmarks.
  • Advanced Model Architecture: Developed a novel framework combining affordance-based reinforcement learning with LLM-derived state representations, exceeding performance benchmarks traditionally reliant on prompt engineering and supervised fine-tuning.
  • Dataset and Embeddings: Leveraged pre-trained LLMs to obtain high-quality embeddings from the Amazon Beauty dataset, employing fastText embeddings to optimize computational resources compared to LLama2.
  • Performance Metrics: Utilized comprehensive evaluation metrics, including Top-K Hit Rate (HR@k) and Normalized Discounted Cumulative Gain (NDCG@k), to rigorously benchmark model performance.
  • Training Methodologies: Experimented extensively with various traing approaches, including warm-start techniques, randomized experiments, and greedy action strategies, showcasing competitive results.

Results

  • Achieved results on par with supervised fine-tuned LLama-7B and approximately two-thirds the performance of supervised fine-tuned ChatGLM models.
  • Significantly reduced training data requirements, demonstrating efficient scalability

Impact

Illustrated that affordance-based DQN models offer a robust and resource-efficient alternative capable of competing with current state-of-the-art supervised fine-tuned LLMs in sequential recommendation tasks, particularly when provided with additional training episodes.
