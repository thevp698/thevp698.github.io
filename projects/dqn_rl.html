Ablation Studies on Deep Q-Learning

View Repository

Overview

Performed comprehensive ablation studies on the Deep Q-Network (DQN) algorithm using two distinct environments: Cartpole (Classic Control) and Lunar Lander (Box2D). This research explored the internal dynamics and critical components of DQN by systematically removing elements such as the target network, replay memory, and exploration mechanisms.

Key Contributions

  • Component-wise Analysis: Investigated the impact of individually removing key DQN components-target networks, memory replay, and epsilon-based exploration-on overall performances. Highlighted the integral roles of these components through rigorous empirical testing.
  • Training Stability Techniques: Implemented soft updates for network weights, effectively reducing variance and stabilizing the learning process. Utilized an epsilon decay algorithm to balance exploration and exploitation dynamically, illustrating its critical importance for consistent reward outcomes.
  • Cross-Environment Comparison: Demonstrated through experimental results that employing identical agents across different environments can be suboptimal, advocating tailored approaches to each distinct environment.
  • Insights on Network Architectures: Observed that using only a policy network can yield strong results with sufficient training episodes, emphasizing the viability of simplified models under certain conditions.

Outcomes

  • Identified memory replay and exploration mechanisms as essential components for DQN effectiveness
  • Recommended adaptive strategies, such as soft weight updates and tailored agents, to ensure robuts and consistent learning performance.
