Adaptive Time-Step Analysis for Coupled Nonlinear PDEs using FEM

View Repository

Overview

Solved complex sets of coupled, nonlinear, time-dependent parabolic partial differential equations (PDEs) using a combination of Forward Euler prediction and Backward Euler correction algorithms. Integrated adaptive time-step control with the Galerkin Finite Element Method (FEM) to effectively model dynamic reaction kinetics.

Key Contributions

  • Numerical Method Development: Implemented coupled PDE solutions using advanced numerical schemes (Forward Euler Predictor and Backward Euler Corrector). Incorporated adaptive time-stepping to accommodate varying reaction rates effectively.
  • Adaptive Time-Step Control: Designed algorithms to adjust time-step size dynamically based on reaction kinetics, specifically observing the impacts of activation energy. Demonstrated that faster reaction kinetics necessitated smaller adaptive time-steps for increased solution accuracy.
  • Reaction Kinetics Analysis: Studied reaction kinetics characterized by slow-fast-slow reaction mechanisms. Conducted detailed analyses of concentration and time effects across varying spatial distances, showcasing the critical relationship between reaction rates and time-step selection.
  • Mesh Independence and Element Size Study: Performed experiments demonstrating that element size variations had minimal effects on time-step adaptations, highlighting the stability of the numerical scheme across mesh configurations.

Outcomes

  • Achieved robust and accurate modelling of complex reaction kinetics through adaptive numerical methods.
  • Developed insights into optimizing computational resources by adaptively controlling time-step sizes based on dynamic reaction characteristics.
